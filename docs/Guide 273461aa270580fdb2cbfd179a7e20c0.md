# Guide

## Team Structure & Roles

### Core Team

Our team operates as a unified orchestra, with each member contributing specialized expertise while maintaining awareness of the bigger picture.

## Communication Standards

### Response Requirements

**24-Hour Rule**: All messages must be acknowledged within 24 hours during business days.

**Immediate Scenarios** (respond within 2 hours):

- Urgent technical issues blocking others
- Meeting schedule changes
- Critical project decisions
- Customer-facing problems

**Standard Response**: "Got it, will respond fully by [specific time]"

### Meeting Protocols

**Punctuality Standards:**

- Join meetings 2-3 minutes early when possible
- If running late, message the team immediately with ETA
- For emergencies requiring absence, provide minimum 2-hour notice when possible

**Meeting Best Practices:**

- Come prepared with relevant materials
- Share agenda items 24 hours in advance for key meetings
- Take turns facilitating meetings to build leadership skills
- End with clear action items and owners

## Work Standards & Accountability

### Project Commitment Framework

**When You Accept Work:**

1. Confirm understanding of requirements and timeline
2. Identify potential risks or dependencies immediately
3. Provide realistic effort estimates
4. Commit to regular progress updates

**Progress Communication:**

- **Weekly**: Share progress updates in team channels
- **Blockers**: Report immediately when discovered
- **Delays**: Communicate as soon as timeline risks become apparent
- **Early Wins**: Share partial work to demonstrate progress

### Quality Standards

**Code & Technical Work:**

- Follow established coding standards and review processes
- Document complex decisions and architectural choices
- Test your work thoroughly before sharing
- Seek feedback early on significant changes

**Documentation & Communication:**

- Write clearly and concisely
- Include context and reasoning behind decisions
- Keep team documentation current
- Share learnings from research or problem-solving

## Work-Life Balance & Sustainability

### Sustainable Pace

We're building revolutionary technology, but we're in this for the long term. Maintain a sustainable pace that allows for:

- Deep, focused work during core hours
- Time for learning and skill development
- Personal life and recuperation
- Creative thinking and innovation

## Performance & Growth

### Individual Development

**Monthly Growth Goals:**

- Set one technical skill development target
- Identify one process or efficiency improvement
- Choose one way to contribute beyond your core responsibilities
- Regular feedback sessions with leadership

**Knowledge Sharing:**

- Present learnings to the team quarterly
- Contribute to technical documentation
- Mentor newer team members when possible
- Stay current with industry trends relevant to your role

### Team Performance Metrics

**What We Measure:**

- Delivery consistency and quality
- Team collaboration effectiveness
- Customer satisfaction and adoption
- Innovation and technical advancement

**What We Don't Measure:**

- Hours worked (we care about outcomes)
- Individual competition (we succeed together)
- Perfection (we value progress and learning)
- Micromanagement metrics (we trust professionals)

## Success Recognition

### Celebrating Wins

- Acknowledge both individual achievements and team successes
- Share customer feedback and positive outcomes
- Recognize behind-the-scenes contributions that enable others
- Celebrate learning and growth, not just perfect execution

---

***Remember**: We're building something revolutionary together. These guidelines exist to help us work effectively as a team while creating the future of software development. When in doubt, choose the approach that best serves our shared mission and values.*
